Japanese Mille Crepe Cake

Go back

Description

Mille crepe cake is a French-inspired dessert that has become popular in Japan. The cake is made up of layers of thin crepes and a light, fluffy cream filling. This recipe calls for a matcha-flavored filling, which gives the cake a lovely green color and a subtle tea flavor.

Ingredients

  • For the crepes:
  • 6 eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • For the filling:
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon matcha powder

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk.
  2. Add the flour, sugar, melted butter, and salt to the bowl and whisk until smooth.
  3. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and grease with cooking spray.
  4. Pour a small amount of batter into the pan and swirl to coat the bottom.
  5. Cook the crepe for 1-2 minutes on each side or until golden brown.
  6. Repeat with the remaining batter to make 20-25 crepes.
  7. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, sugar, and matcha powder until stiff peaks form.
  8. Place a crepe on a plate and spread a thin layer of the matcha cream on top.
  9. Repeat with the remaining crepes and cream, stacking the crepes on top of each other.
  10. Refrigerate the cake for at least 2 hours before serving.
